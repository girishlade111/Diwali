About Diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most significant festivals in Hinduism. It symbolizes the spiritual victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance.

History & Origins

Ancient Traditions of Light

Celebrating the timeless heritage of Diwali through generations

Return of Lord Rama

The most popular legend tells of Lord Rama's return to Ayodhya after 14 years of exile and his victory over the demon king Ravana. The people of Ayodhya lit oil lamps to welcome their beloved king.

Goddess Lakshmi

Diwali is also associated with Goddess Lakshmi, the deity of wealth and prosperity. It's believed that she visits clean and well-lit homes on this auspicious night.

Lord Krishna & Narakasura

In South India, Diwali commemorates Lord Krishna's victory over the demon Narakasura, freeing 16,000 women from his captivity.

Ancient Harvest Festival

Historically, Diwali marked the end of harvest season and the beginning of winter. Farmers would celebrate their prosperity and pray for continued abundance.

Sacred Traditions

Cleaning & Decoration

Homes are thoroughly cleaned and decorated with rangoli patterns, flowers, and lights to welcome Goddess Lakshmi.

Lighting Diyas

Oil lamps (diyas) are lit to dispel darkness and ignorance, symbolizing the inner light that protects from spiritual darkness.

Lakshmi Puja

Special prayers are offered to Goddess Lakshmi and Lord Ganesha for prosperity, wealth, and the removal of obstacles.

Gift Exchange

Families and friends exchange sweets, gifts, and good wishes, strengthening bonds and spreading joy.

Fireworks

Colorful fireworks light up the sky, adding to the festive atmosphere and celebrating the victory of light.

Rangoli Art

Intricate patterns made with colored powders, rice, or flower petals adorn doorways and courtyards.

Five Days of Celebration

1

Dhanteras

The first day dedicated to wealth and prosperity. People buy gold, silver, and new utensils.

2

Naraka Chaturdashi

Also known as Choti Diwali, commemorating Krishna's victory over Narakasura.

3

Lakshmi Puja

The main Diwali day when Goddess Lakshmi is worshipped and diyas are lit everywhere.

4

Govardhan Puja

Celebrates Krishna lifting Mount Govardhan to protect villagers from Indra's wrath.

5

Bhai Dooj

Celebrates the bond between brothers and sisters, similar to Raksha Bandhan.
